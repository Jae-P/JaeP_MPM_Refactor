Publishing Company Setup

Why set up a Publisher?

Own/control your catalog, collect the publisher’s share of royalties, and license your songs for sync.

Business Structure

Start as a DBA or form an LLC for liability protection and clean banking/taxes.

Royalties Overview

Performance (PRO), Mechanical (HFA/Music Reports), and Sync (direct licenses). Make sure each lane is covered.

  1. Choose 2–3 name options for your publishing company (PROs ask for alternates).
  2. Register your business (DBA, LLC, or Corporation) with your state.
  3. Get an EIN from the IRS.
  4. Open a dedicated business bank account.

Performance Rights Organizations (PROs)

What’s a PRO?

PROs (BMI/ASCAP) collect performance royalties when your music is publicly played (radio, live, TV, streaming).

Writer vs Publisher

Open both accounts: the writer share pays you as a songwriter; the publisher share pays your company.

IPI/CAE Number

Once joined, you’ll receive an IPI/CAE identifier. Use it on all work registrations to ensure correct payment.

1) Create Accounts

Register each song with accurate splits, ISWC if available, and correct contributors.

3) Ongoing Updates

Update new releases, new collaborators, set lists for live shows, and cue sheets if applicable.
